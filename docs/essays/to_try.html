Endeavour. The name of a ship used by James Cook to chart New Zealand and Australia, 1769 - 1771. Endeavour is glorified, but in retrospect doesn't follow a positive path for many, or most.

Not to be confused with Endurance, Shackleton's 1912 Arctic exploration vessel, lost when it became stuck in the ice floes, crushed and abandoned. [1]

So far it seems endeavour is some combination of effort and endurance. And do Americans really spell it endeavor? In Yoda's philisophy, [relating doing or not doing to trying].
